Excerpted passages relevant to the Literacy Corps, Health Corps, and Reconstruction/Development (Extension) Corps, WITH original inline citation markers preserved so the underlying primary/secondary source of each figure is traceable:

--- Enumerated 19-point program (numbering per article) ---

"Formation of the Literacy Corps", so that those who had a high school diploma and were required to serve their country as soldiers could do so by fighting illiteracy in the villages. [ref name=":0", i.e. Encyclopedia.com "White Revolution (1961-1963)", https://www.encyclopedia.com/humanities/encyclopedias-almanacs-transcripts-and-maps/white-revolution-1961-1963] In 1963, approximately 2/3 of the population was illiterate, with the remaining third found mainly in the capital city of Tehran.

"Formation of the Health Corps" to extend public health care throughout the villages and rural regions of Iran. [ref :0] In 3 years, almost 4,500 medical groups were trained; nearly 10 million cases were treated by the Corps.

"Formation of the Reconstruction and Development Corps" to teach the villagers the modern methods and techniques of farming and keeping livestock. [ref :0] Agricultural production between 1964 and 1970 increased by 80% in tonnage and 67% in value.

"Urban and Rural Modernization and Reconstruction" with the help of the Reconstruction and Development Corps. Building of public baths, schools and libraries; installing water pumps and power generators for running water and electricity. [same source thread]

--- Education section ---

"Free and Compulsory Education" and a daily free meal for all children from kindergarten to 14 years of age. Primary schools were built in hundreds of villages that previously did not have one. [ref :0] In 1978, 25% of Iranians were enrolled in public schools alone. In that same year there were 185,000 students of both sexes studying in Iran's universities. In addition to the above there were over 100,000 students pursuing their studies abroad, of which 50,000 were enrolled in colleges and universities in the United States.

Educational institutions also grew after the launching of the White Revolution. Enrollment in kindergarten increased from 13,300 to 221,990, elementary schools from 1,640,000 to 4,080,000, secondary schools from 370,000 to 741,000 and colleges from 24,885 to 145,210. [uncited in Wikipedia article as of retrieval -- flagged, treat as unverified/needs independent confirmation] The Literacy Corps also helped raise the literacy rate from 26 to 42 percent. [also uncited in the Wikipedia article text itself; NOTE this literacy-rate range (26%->42%) is NOT the same figures as the Encyclopaedia Iranica LITERACY CORPS article's illiteracy rates (67.2%/87.8% male/female illiterate in 1966 -> 44.2%/53% in 1979) -- the two sources are not measuring identical things (overall literacy rate vs sex-disaggregated illiteracy rate, and different endpoint years), both are preserved here without reconciliation because reconciling them would require guessing.]

--- Land reform (context, not corps-specific but same program) ---

National referendum, 26 January 1963: 5,598,711 voted for reforms, 4,115 voted against (boycotted by opposition). [ref: Milani, Moshen M., The Making of Iran's Islamic Revolution, Westview Press, 1988, p.85]

Forestry: More than 9 million trees planted in 26 regions, creating 70,000 acres (280 km^2) of "green belts". [ref :0]

Land reform redistributed land to approximately 2.5 million families (long-run figure) [[ref: Britannica "White Revolution (Iran)"]]; an earlier passage in the same article states redistribution to "approximately 1.5 million families" affecting "approximately nine million people, or 40% of Iran's population" [uncited in current article text].

Economic growth: annual economic growth rate averaging 9.8% in the period; over one million families became small business owners; an estimated 700,000 salaried professionals. [ref: Maloney, Suzanne, "1979: Iran and America," Brookings, 24 January 2019]
