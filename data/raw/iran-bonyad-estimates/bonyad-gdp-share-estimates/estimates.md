# Bonyads' / IRGC-Affiliated Enterprises' Share of Iran's GDP — Full Range of Estimates

This is a **contested-estimate** compilation, not a single statistical series. Estimates of what
share of Iran's GDP is controlled by bonyads (parastatal religious/revolutionary foundations),
the IRGC's business empire, and the combined "military-bonyad complex" vary enormously — from
roughly **4% to 65%** — depending on (a) which entities are counted (bonyads narrowly vs. bonyads
+ IRGC + military institutions combined), (b) whether "control" means formal ownership/reported
revenue vs. estimated total economic footprint including informal/black-market activity, and
(c) who is doing the estimating (the Iranian government itself has strong incentive to report a
low number; opposition-aligned and some Western analysts have incentive to report a high number).
Per project policy (docs/bookkeeping.md, "Source reliability & neutrality principles"), the full
range is recorded here with every source attributed — no single number is picked as "the" answer.

Retrieved: 2026-07-12. All entries below were fetched via WebFetch/browser directly from the
cited URL on that date unless otherwise noted.

## The estimates, low to high

- **4.2% of GDP (down from 8.2% in 2020)** — Mohammad Javad Iravani, head of the supervision and
  audit department in the Office of the Supreme Leader (Ayatollah Ali Khamenei), speaking January
  23, 2023. Scope: "Revolutionary organs" / state-controlled holdings and trusts not run by the
  administrative government ("bonyads" narrowly defined). Iravani also stated bonyad assets equal
  9.5% of total state-company assets and 5.7% of state-company revenues, and that bonyads provide
  45% of investment in development projects in impoverished regions.
  **Source:** PressTV (Iranian state media), "Size of bonyads in Iran's economy halved despite US
  propaganda," Jan 23, 2023.
  URL: https://www.presstv.ir/Detail/2023/01/23/696866/Iran-bonyads-share-economy-Iravani-US-propaganda
  **Reliability label per project rules: state-media-attributed, official Iranian government
  figure.** This is the lowest estimate in the range and comes from the party with the strongest
  incentive to minimize the reported number; treat as the "official" baseline, not a neutral
  academic estimate, and cross-check against independent figures below.

- **20% of Iran's GDP** — Afshin Molavi, *The Soul of Iran* (W.W. Norton, 2005/2006), p.176.
  General mid-2000s estimate of bonyads' overall economic weight (bonyads described as "second
  only to the oil industry in manufacturing, trading, and real estate development").
  **Source (as cited by):** Wikipedia, "Bonyad" article, footnote [1].
  URL: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bonyad

- **Sector-specific concentration (not an overall GDP %, included for context on market power):**
  the Bonyad-e Mostazafan va Janbazan (Foundation for the Oppressed and Disabled) alone controls
  "20% of the country's production of textiles, 40% of soft drinks, two-thirds of all glass
  products," plus a dominant share of tiles, chemicals, tires, and foodstuffs.
  **Source:** NHH Sam 2007, "Destructive Competition," as cited by Wikipedia "Bonyad," footnote
  [11]. URL: https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bonyad

- **"Over 50%" of Iran's GDP** — IRGC-affiliated parastatal corporations and organizations
  specifically (not counting the broader bonyad network separately). Stated as "generally
  believed" / widely cited figure in a March 2026 policy piece.
  **Source:** Henri Van der Veken, "The IRGC, the Iranian Economy, and Prospects for Regime
  Change," Centre for International Security & Economic Strategy (CISES, London-based independent
  research centre), March 24, 2026.
  URL: https://cises.org/2026/03/24/the-irgc-the-iranian-economy-and-prospects-for-regime-change/
  The same article separately notes: "some estimates attribute **a third to two thirds** [~33%
  to ~67%] of the country's GDP to IRGC-affiliated companies and organisations," describing the
  range as an acknowledgment of how contested/opaque the true figure is.

- **"More than 50% of the country's GDP in 2013"** — the "military-bonyad complex" (bonyads +
  IRGC + other military/security institutions combined), described as the author's own
  calculation.
  **Source:** Kayhan Valadbaygi (Research Fellow, International Institute of Social History,
  Amsterdam; author of *Capitalism in Contemporary Iran*, Manchester University Press, 2024),
  "Beyond the IRGC: The rise of Iran's military-bonyad complex," Clingendael Institute (Netherlands
  Institute of International Relations), October 2, 2025.
  URL: https://www.clingendael.org/publication/beyond-irgc-rise-irans-military-bonyad-complex

- **"Around two-thirds" (~67%) of the economy** — IRGC economic control, stated in an interview.
  **Source:** Rachel Ehrenfeld (director, American Center for Democracy and the Economic Warfare
  Institute), interviewed by RFE/RL, published April 21, 2026.
  URL: https://www.rferl.org/a/iran-us-irgc-ships-economy-entreched-shadow/33736577.html
  **Caveat:** Ehrenfeld's affiliated organizations are explicitly advocacy-oriented (regime-change
  focused); this figure is her personal assessment ("from the last data that I could actually
  surmise") given in an interview, not a cited study. Included because it was published by RFE/RL
  (a US government-funded international broadcaster, not an MEK/NCRI-type outlet), but it should
  be read as an advocacy-affiliated analyst's opinion, not an independently verified statistic.

- **60% to 65% of Iranian GDP** — bonyads collectively, "when combined with military and
  non-military state foundations and IRGC-affiliated enterprises" (i.e., the broadest possible
  scope: all parastatal/foundation-linked economic activity).
  **Source:** GlobalSecurity.org, "Iran -- Enduring Islamic Government: Political-Military Economy
  and Proxy Architecture" (bonyad system section), 2026.
  URL: https://www.globalsecurity.org/military/world/iran/continuity-polmil.htm
  This is the highest and broadest-scope estimate in this collection.

## Estimates found but deliberately excluded from the list above

- An IFMAT.org article headlined "Iranian Bonyads make up approximately 30% of Iran's GDP" was
  found via search but is **not included as a citable estimate**. IFMAT ("Iranian, Frauds,
  Manipulations, Atrocities...") self-describes its mission as exposing and prosecuting the
  Iranian government, i.e. it is an advocacy/opposition outlet in the same category the project's
  source-reliability rules ask us to treat with the same skepticism as MEK/NCRI-affiliated sites
  (docs/bookkeeping.md: "the same standard would apply to any outlet, of any political stripe,
  with a comparable record"). Its ~30% figure was not independently verified and is omitted rather
  than risk citing unverified advocacy-outlet data; it falls within the range already established
  by the sources above (4.2%–65%) in any case.
- The Wikipedia article "Economic activities of the Iranian Revolutionary Guard Corps"
  (https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Economic_activities_of_the_Iranian_Revolutionary_Guard_Corps)
  carries an active Wikipedia editorial warning as of retrieval: "This article may incorporate
  text from a large language model, which is prohibited in Wikipedia articles. It may include
  hallucinated information, copyright violations, claims not verified in cited sources, original
  research, or fictitious references" (banner dated November 2025). A widely-circulated summary
  claim from this page/its search snippets ("estimates... vary from ten percent to over 50[%]")
  was therefore **not** included as a sourced data point here, since the page's own reliability is
  flagged by Wikipedia itself. Flagging this explicitly so a future researcher doesn't re-add it
  without independent verification.
- A Reuters wire story ("Iranian Guards' business empire to win big if U.S. sanctions lifted,"
  June 20, 2026, republished by The Express Tribune and Al-Monitor) discusses the IRGC's business
  empire at length but was checked and does **not** contain a specific GDP-share percentage of its
  own — only qualitative descriptions ("multibillion-dollar trade networks") sourced to unnamed
  Iranian officials. Not included as a numeric estimate for that reason.

## Why the range is so wide (methodological notes, not a project conclusion)

- **Scope differences** are the single biggest driver: narrow "bonyads only" figures (Iravani 4.2%,
  Molavi 20%) are far below combined "bonyads + IRGC + military" figures (Clingendael, CISES,
  GlobalSecurity.org, 50%–65%), because the IRGC's own business empire (Khatam al-Anbiya, Sepah
  Cooperative Foundation, Basij Cooperative Foundation, etc.) is sometimes counted as a separate
  "bonyad" and sometimes not.
- **Formal vs. informal accounting.** The Iranian government's own 4.2%/9.5%/5.7% figures
  (Iravani/PressTV) appear to reflect reported/audited state-company asset and revenue shares —
  i.e., what shows up in formal statistics — whereas outside estimates (Clingendael, CISES,
  GlobalSecurity.org) explicitly describe bonyad/IRGC finances as opaque, tax-exempt, and
  deliberately shielded from audit, and are attempting to estimate the *true* economic footprint
  including activity that doesn't appear in official statistics.
- **Political incentives run in both directions.** The Iranian government has an incentive to
  report a low number (to rebut "US propaganda," per Iravani's own framing); Western
  security-policy think tanks and regime-change-oriented advocates have an incentive to report a
  high number (to argue sanctions/military pressure on the IRGC will meaningfully weaken the
  regime's economic base). This is exactly why docs/bookkeeping.md instructs recording the full
  range with attribution rather than selecting a single "best" figure.
