Subject: History of civil aviation in Iran since 1927, structured in six periods:
i. The Formation of the Iranian Air Force (1923-27)
ii. Junkers Airline in Iran (1927-32)
iii. Iranian State Airlines of the Ministry of Post and Telegram (1938-46)
iv. Privately Owned Commercial Airlines (1945-62): (1) Iranian Airways, (2) Persian Air Services, (3) United Iranian Airlines
v. State Owned Commercial Airline / Iran Air (1962-79)
vi. Multiple Commercial Airlines (1979-2007)

KEY QUANTITATIVE / DATED FACTS (verbatim or close paraphrase; full narrative at source URL):

PRE-IRAN AIR ERA:
- First flight over Tehran: end of 1913 (Bleriot XI, Russian pilot, crash-landed at Meydan-e Mashq).
- Iranian Air Force founded 1921-23; first aircraft purchased from Germany 1922 (Junkers F13); 2 more Junkers F13 purchased 1923 with Gilan/Mazandaran provincial revenue.
- Junkers Airline in Iran: 5-year agreement signed 1926; first scheduled air service 8 February 1927 (Tehran-Bandar-e Pahlavi); served ~10 Iranian cities plus Baku, Baghdad, Kabul by 1932; ceased operations 1932.
- Iranian State Airlines (Ministry of Post and Telegram): founded 1938, started with 2 De Havilland DH 89 aircraft, fleet grew to 4.
- Iranian Airways (first private commercial airline): founded December 1944, start-up capital 50 million rial; first 3 C-47/DC-3 aircraft arrived Tehran 17 May 1947; fleet grew to 20 DC-3s at peak; 1952 accident at Mehrabad Airport killed 27 people; by 1958 operated to 14 domestic + 10 regional (Afghanistan/Pakistan/India/Iraq/Saudi Arabia/Dubai/Kuwait) destinations, plus cargo to Ankara/Milan/Zurich/Frankfurt; added 3 Vickers Viscount turboprops in 1958.
- Persian Air Services (PAS): founded 1954 as a cargo airline by Ahmad Shafiq.
- United Iranian Airlines: founded August 1961 (govt 51%, Iranian Airways 20%, PAS 20%, public 9%); collapsed almost immediately.

IRAN AIR / HOMA (1962-79):
- Founded February 1962 by parliamentary decree, absorbing Iranian Airways + Persian Air Services. Start-up capital: 170 million rial (50 million cash + 120 million in airplanes).
- Managing director 1962-1978: Major General (later Lieutenant General, 1966) Ali Mohammad Khademi, previously Chief of Staff of the Iranian Air Force. Iran Air remained profitable without subsidy every year until 1979 under his 17-year tenure.
- Workforce grew "from 700 to over 12,000 skilled personnel" over the Khademi era (1962-1978/79).
- 1970: Khademi became the first Asian president of IATA (International Air Transportation Association).
- Fleet modernization: transitioned to an all-Boeing jet fleet -- 29 Boeing aircraft (737, 727-100, 727-200, 707, 747-100, 747-200, 747SP) -- then grew to 35 all-jet aircraft after adding 6 Airbus aircraft. "At this stage the company carried close to five million passengers a year" [year not explicitly stated in article text, but contextually placed after the Airbus acquisition, i.e. later 1970s].
- Company's 15-year plan covered 1975-1990 (fleet replacement plan).
- Concorde: British Concorde flew to Tehran June 1972 during a 30-day demonstration tour; Iran Air's own experts advised against purchase; the Shah, despite personal interest, cancelled the order.
- International expansion: first Iran Air flight to New York JFK on 15 May 1975; also served Beijing and Tokyo in the 1970s, with offices opened in Bangkok, Manila, Singapore in preparation for further expansion. US route service terminated 4 November 1979 (hostage crisis).
- Direct flights to Europe/Persian Gulf were also operated out of Abadan and Shiraz (decentralized regional hubs), not just Tehran.
- Ticket discount policy: 40% discount for students and government employees/families to broaden access to international air travel beyond the wealthy.
- Diversification: Homa Hotel Group; airline subsidiary Iran Air Tours founded in the 1970s.

POST-1979 (1979-2007 period covered by the article):
- Post-revolution: company renamed "Iran Air, the Airline of the Islamic Republic of Iran"; mass turnover/loss of experienced executives and middle-managers for political reasons.
- US sanctions crippled maintenance access after the 1979-81 hostage crisis and broader collapse of US-Iran relations.
- First fatal post-revolution accident: 21 January 1980, 129 lives lost.
- After the 1988 Iran-Iraq War ceasefire, demand for air travel surged; the government lowered the airline-licensing bar to a 2 billion rial investment, and at least 14 new Iranian airlines were founded in short order -- most using obsolete ex-Soviet aircraft, with declining safety/service quality.
- Most disastrous accident: 3 July 1988, Iran Air Flight 655 (Airbus A300) shot down over the Persian Gulf, 290 people killed.
